What Drives Your Multiple UP

  • Recurring service agreements - Monthly or annual maintenance contracts create predictable cash flow. Buyers pay a premium for revenue they can count on.
  • Multiple trucks and crews - Capacity without the owner in a van. More trucks = more scale potential = higher multiple.
  • Licensed technicians on staff - Not subs. W-2 employees with plumbing licenses. Buyers want to know the workforce stays after closing.
  • Documented SOPs - Every job type, every process, written down. Dispatch, estimating, collections - all systematized.
  • Low owner dependency - You take 4 weeks off and the business doesn't skip a beat. This alone can add 1-2x to your multiple.

What Kills Your Multiple

  • Owner does the selling - If you're the one closing every deal and managing every key relationship, the business dies when you leave.
  • Customer concentration - If one builder or property manager accounts for more than 15-20% of revenue, buyers see a ticking time bomb.
  • No maintenance contracts - 100% project-based revenue means zero predictability. That's the difference between 4x and 7x.
  • No second-in-command - No operations manager, no lead tech who can run jobs. Buyers need to see a team, not a one-man show.

Why Plumbing? Four Reasons.

  1. Fragmented market There are over 120,000 plumbing businesses in the U.S. Most are under $5M revenue. PE firms see consolidation opportunity everywhere.
  2. Recurring revenue potential Drain cleaning, maintenance contracts, water heater replacements - plumbing has natural recurring revenue that most owners aren't capturing.

  4. Aging infrastructure U.S. water infrastructure is graded D+ by the American Society of Civil Engineers. Demand isn't going away for decades.
